# MuleSoft Content Workflow - Professional Audio Recording Script

## Recording Instructions

### Setup Requirements
- **Microphone:** USB condenser mic (Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica AT2020) or professional setup
- **Environment:** Quiet room, minimal echo
- **Recording Software:** Audacity, GarageBand, Adobe Audition, or similar
- **Sample Rate:** 44.1 kHz
- **Bit Depth:** 16-bit or 24-bit
- **Format:** Export as WAV or MP3 (192 kbps+)

### Recording Tips
- **Pace:** Moderate, clear enunciation (~140 words per minute)
- **Tone:** Professional, confident, enthusiastic but not overly excited
- **Breathing:** Pause naturally at commas and periods
- **Pronunciation:** Clear distinction between similar terms
- **Energy:** Maintain consistent energy throughout
- **Practice:** Read through script 2-3 times before recording

## Recording Session Checklist

### Pre-Recording
- [ ] Read through entire script 3 times
- [ ] Practice pronunciation of technical terms
- [ ] Warm up voice (vocal exercises, hydration)
- [ ] Test microphone levels (aim for -12dB to -6dB)
- [ ] Check room acoustics (no echo)
- [ ] Eliminate background noise (AC, notifications off)
- [ ] Have water nearby

### During Recording
- [ ] Record in one take if possible (consistency)
- [ ] If mistake, pause, breath, re-record from last paragraph
- [ ] Maintain consistent distance from microphone (6-8 inches)
- [ ] Monitor levels (shouldn't peak/clip)
- [ ] Record 2-3 full takes for options

### Post-Recording
- [ ] Listen to entire recording
- [ ] Remove background noise (noise reduction)
- [ ] Normalize audio levels (-3dB to -1dB)
- [ ] Add slight compression (3:1 ratio, -10dB threshold)
- [ ] EQ if needed (boost clarity around 3-5kHz)
- [ ] Export as WAV (lossless) and MP3 (192 kbps+)
- [ ] Test sync with demo

## Pro Recording Tips

### Voice Techniques
- **Smile while speaking:** Creates warmer, more engaging tone
- **Stand while recording:** Better breath support and energy
- **Use hand gestures:** Even though not on camera, helps with natural delivery
- **Record in the morning:** Voice is typically clearer

### Technical Quality
- **Room Treatment:** Hang blankets or use a closet for better acoustics
- **Pop Filter:** Reduces plosives (P, B sounds)
- **Audio Interface:** Better quality than direct USB if available
- **Headphones:** Monitor recording quality in real-time

### Professional Polish
- **De-esser:** Reduce harsh S sounds in post-production
- **Limiter:** Prevent any unexpected peaks
- **Room Tone:** Record 10 seconds of silence for editing
- **Master:** Final pass for consistency and loudness normalization
